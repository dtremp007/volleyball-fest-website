# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Volleyball Fest serves two connected audiences. Public visitors are players, captains, and local supporters in Cuauhtemoc who need league information quickly: teams, standings, schedules, signup status, and match results. Admin users are league operators managing seasons, team registration, groups, schedules, scores, exports, and public content.

## Product Purpose

Volleyball Fest exists to make a recreational volleyball league easier to run and easier to follow. The public site should make the league feel active, local, and trustworthy. The admin area should reduce operational friction across the season lifecycle: open signup, organize teams, generate and adjust schedules, enter scores, and publish reliable standings.

Success means captains can understand what is happening without asking an admin, and admins can move from season setup to live operations without fighting spreadsheets, duplicated data, or unclear workflow state.

## Brand Personality

Clear, energetic, local.

The interface should feel lively enough for a community sports league, but not noisy. Public pages can carry more volleyball energy and event presence. Admin screens should stay practical, legible, and calm under repetitive operational work.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS dashboards that could belong to any company. Avoid overhyped pro-sports branding, aggressive stadium graphics, and visual noise that makes a recreational league feel inflated. Avoid cluttered tournament sheets, dense spreadsheet mimicry, and decorative effects that slow down admin work.

## Design Principles

1. Make the current season obvious. League work happens inside a season context, so navigation, labels, and workflow state should keep that context visible.
2. Publish confidence, not clutter. Public pages should answer the next obvious question: who plays, when, where, and how the standings look.
3. Keep admin workflows direct. Setup, scheduling, score entry, and exports should prioritize scanning, comparison, and repeated action over decorative presentation.
4. Respect the local league voice. Public copy is Spanish for players and visitors; admin copy is English for operators. Do not mix languages within a surface.
5. Use energy with restraint. The brand can feel athletic and social, but the product should never obscure data, controls, or task progress.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Use WCAG-conscious contrast, keyboard-friendly controls, visible focus states, and reduced-motion support for nonessential animation. Public surfaces should remain readable on mobile in outdoor or gym-lighting conditions. Admin surfaces should support dense workflows without relying on color alone to communicate status.
